PTSD diagnosis

Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders-V; a subset of criteria for PTSD diagnosis includes the following three conditions: PTSD can be triggered by an event that involves threatened death or a threat to one’s physical integrity; a person’s response to the event involves intense fear, helplessness, or horror; and in the aftermath of the trauma, the person feels as if the traumatic event were recurring, including a sense of re-living the experience.

The field of PTSD is currently challenged by fluctuations in diagnostic criteria, have implications for epidemiological, biological, genetic and treatment studies.
