Word Sense Disambiguation

Word sense disambiguation (WSD) is the problem to identify which sense of a word is used in a sentence. In standard format, the problem is written below.

ENGLISH WORD SENSE DISAMBIGUATION

INSTANCE: English text x from English alphabet, a word y appearing in x

QUESTION: which meaning of y is used in x

WSD Approaches -- RNN-LSTM Model

WSD is the fundamental but one of the most challenging tasks in Natural Language Processing (NLP), for the reasons of data sparseness and complex inherent language lexicons [1]. The approaches to WSD include supervised approaches and knowledge-based approaches. The current recurrent neural networks (RNNs) demonstrates suitable for feature extraction and solve all-words lexical disambiguation [2]. These and other sources will be used to solve WSD problem successfully.

Datasets for LSTM Model Training

In total, there are 4 datasets applied in the model. The 2 annotated datasets SemCor and OMSTI were used to generate sense embeddings. The unannotated Gigaword data were used to generate language model. The pre-organized synsets from WordNet were used as dictionary and provided the final correct word sense.

SemCor (Miller et al., 1994). SemCor is a manually sense-annotated corpus divided in 352 documents for a total of 226,040 sense annotations.

OMSTI (Taghipour and Ng, 2015a). OMSTI (One Million Sense-Tagged Instances) is a large corpus annotated with senses from the WordNet 3.0 inventory.

English Gigaword (https://catalog.ldc.upenn.edu/LDC2003T05), a corpus of 1.75 B words.

WordNet 3.1 (https://wordnet.princeton.edu/) : a large lexical database of English in which words are sets of cognitive synonyms (synsets).
